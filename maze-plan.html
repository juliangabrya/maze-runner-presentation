The 5×5 maze the robot solves

This is the exact maze shown in the live demo. It's a "perfect" maze (no loops), so the left-hand rule is guaranteed to walk it from START to GOAL — every time. Build it roughly to this plan; the robot doesn't need exact measurements, just solid, consistent walls.

START — robot faces ▸ (East) GOAL solution path

A What you need

  • ~16 books or boxes for the walls — anything solid, at least 12 cm tall so the sensors always hit a wall, not over it.
  • A flat floor about 1.5 m × 1.5 m.
  • Tape or chalk to mark the 5×5 grid first (optional but helps).

B How to build it

  • Mark a 5×5 grid of squares, each roughly 30 cm wide — about one big book-length. They don't have to be perfect, just about the same size.
  • Walk the diagram cell by cell. Wherever the plan shows a black line, stand a book/box to make a wall. Wherever there's a gap, leave it open — that's a corridor.
  • Keep walls solid and continuous with no big gaps — the laser sensors need a surface to bounce off. Matte / non-shiny surfaces read best.
  • Put the robot in the START cell (top-left) facing right (East), as the green arrow shows.

C What the robot will do

From START it keeps its “left hand” on the wall. Its first move: it senses a wall ahead and an opening to its right, so it turns right and drives on. The full sequence of turns to the goal is:

ROUTE  →  R F F F L L R L R R F L

(R = right · L = left · F = forward · B = back, relative to the way it's facing.)

One setting to check on the robot: the route is 12 decisions long, so make sure MAX_MOVES is set to at least 12 (e.g. 15) — otherwise it will stop early and radio a partial route. Everything else can stay as tuned.

D If it struggles on the day

  • Drifts into a wall: nudge the MOTOR_BIAS a little; walls being uneven width is the usual cause.
  • Misses an opening: the corridor is probably wider than expected — narrow it, or it sees “open” where you meant a wall.
  • Stops short: raise MAX_MOVES. Loops or backtracks oddly: a wall has a gap the sensor sees through — close it.
  • Worst case, show the on-screen demo (it solves this exact maze) and talk through it — the audience still sees the algorithm work.
